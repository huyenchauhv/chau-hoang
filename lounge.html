The Lounge

The Lounge project is inspired by the activities that have long taken place at the Hanoi Children’s Palace, a space for learning, playing, and fostering a sense of community for generations of Hanoi’s children.


The project proposes an experiential area in which the everyday activities of children are reinterpreted through the works of artists whose practices have often incorporated a certain degree of playfulness, participatory elements, and the experience of getting to know one’s surroundings with open arms, eyes, and minds.


The entire fifth floor of the functional building — originally a row of English classrooms — is reconfigured into six rooms: Weaving Room, Reading Room, Empty Room, Music Room, Robot Room, and The Lounge. Artworks, anchored and dispersed throughout the space, functioning both as semantic counterparts to the activities taking place and as anchors for the overall spatial composition and atmosphere.


In The Lounge, viewing, interacting with the artworks, and participating in the process of artistic creation are all part of the experience. Art, like life, is about breathing, playing, watching, and resting — ever-present in both the hustle and bustle as well as in moments of stillness.

Curatorially, the project approached exhibition-making not as a display of individual artworks but as a shared environment constructed through activities, participation, and spatial experience.


Structure

Weaving Room
Home to Little Looms, a workshop led by artist Nguyễn Quốc Thành that explores sewing, mending, embroidery, and weaving in relation to artistic practice. The space is transformed into a series of small “looms” made from found furniture and existing architectural elements, inviting visitors to weave, stitch, and attach materials together in the making of a collective sculptural work.
Reading Room
A reading space built around a selection of publications from the Children's Palace library, arranged with children’s comfort in mind, allowing for different postures and ways of reading. It also includes a listening station featuring excerpts from selected books read by artists, writers, poets, and friends of the project.
Empty Room / Multifunctional Room
An intentionally vacant classroom that retained the appearance of a typical learning space while remaining available for temporary activities and gatherings.
Music Room
A display of self-made musical instruments by Nguyen Nhat Quang, Nguyen Dinh Phuong, and Nguyen Duc Phuong, constructed from materials collected within the Children’s Palace, alongside toys and other everyday objects.
Robot Room
Developed in collaboration with the Children’s Palace STEM Club, featuring moving robots created by its members. The space also hosts a robot-making workshop led by STEAM teacher Dương Thanh Thủy.
The Lounge
A space for waiting, resting, and informal gathering throughout the exhibition.
